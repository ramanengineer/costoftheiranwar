Sources and Methodology

Cost counter: Pentagon briefing to Senate Armed Services Committee (Mar 11, 2026) via NYT. $11.3B for first 6 days. $1B/day ongoing. Sen. Chris Coons (NBC News): "likely an undercount."

Live market data: Yahoo Finance API via allorigins.win/corsproxy.io. Refreshes every 60 minutes. Prices delayed 15–20 min.

Currency rates: open.er-api.com (primary, CORS-enabled, free) with Frankfurter API and proxy fallbacks. GBP, EUR, NZD, AUD, JPY, CHF, INR. Updated hourly.

US Gas & Diesel prices: U.S. EIA open API — weekly national averages. Updated weekly (Mondays).

Fear & Greed index: alternative.me free API — crypto market sentiment. Updated daily.

War Stress Index model: Weighted market-stress calculation — oil price shock (35%), currency basket weakness (25%), S&P 500 performance (20%), VIX elevation (10%), gold safe-haven premium (10%). Base: 15% pre-war. This is a stress indicator, not a formal recession probability model.

Recession odds: Goldman Sachs (30%), EY-Parthenon/Gregory Daco (40%), Moody's Analytics/Mark Zandi (48.6%), JP Morgan/Bob Michele (25%). Bloomberg Mar 25, CBS News, Fortune Mar 25, 2026.

Historical war costs: Watson Institute at Brown University "Costs of War" project. CRS R40727. All figures adjusted to 2026 USD.

Human cost: UNHCR (displacement), UN Human Rights, UNESCO, Hengaw, AP, Reuters. Civilian casualty figures are estimates and likely undercounts.
